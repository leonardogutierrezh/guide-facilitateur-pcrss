# Activité — Entretien et maintenance / Operation & maintenance (Step 14)

> Phase 6 · Activité 4 · Étape 14 · Source : Part 3, §6

## Quick reference (EN)

O&M ensures the sustainability of PCRSS-built infrastructure. **Entretien** (upkeep) = periodic, regular activities to keep an asset in good condition; **maintenance** = periodic actions to keep, prevent damage, slow degradation or restore an asset's function and ensure longevity.

**Why:** ensure sustainability; keep works clean; do repairs in time; the cost of repair/rehabilitation rises over time without good O&M. **Who:** the **CEM** prepares and implements the O&M plan and manages O&M funds; the **community** owns and cares for the asset (routine upkeep, minor repairs, responsible use, prompt reporting); the **B-CDVFQ** approves the plan; the **SDE** handles major/emergency repairs. O&M work may be voluntary or paid — **women should be among the paid workers**; the FT trains workers as needed.

**How:** no one-size-fits-all — water systems need a permanent maintenance team; roads need periodic community work; irrigation is maintained by the farmer-users. **The O&M plan (PEM, form T16)** lists activities, who is responsible, materials/spare parts/tools and costs, annual budget, user fees and how they're collected/managed, and required safeguards — and must integrate climate-risk management. The CEM produces plans for new sub-projects and updates earlier ones.

Step 14 also includes the **final reception (réception définitive)** after the warranty period and lifting of reserves (attestation de parfait achèvement). Assets are recorded in the Commune's asset accounting at final reception; an **affermage contract** (CDVFQ–Mairie–provider) may be signed to ensure durable functioning.
