# Activité — Passation des marchés communautaires / Community procurement (Step 9)

> Phase 4 · Activité 2 · Étape 9 · Source : Part 2, §5

## Quick reference (EN)

Community procurement is how the community selects a provider/contractor to deliver a sub-project (works, supplies, equipment, or non-consultant services). It is done **publicly for transparency** — the whole community sees how much money, the quantity and quality of goods/works/services. It covers specifications, ToR, market study, price search via quotation and negotiation, purchase, and delivery, through contracts.

**Internal actors:** AG (elects the bodies); B-CDVFQ (prepares, launches, receives, opens, evaluates, awards, notifies, signs contracts; keeps the bid-purchase and bid-deposit registers); CC (verifies compliance, procedures, contract clauses, fund use, disbursements); CTMO (follows publication → award → contract; follows implementation); CEO (opens and evaluates bids, proposes award); CGP (registers/handles complaints). **External actors:** SC/FC (advisory only — **must not** act operationally in procurement); FT (helps finalize the DAOL technical aspects, observer at evaluation, controls works); contractor/supplier/consultant (signs and delivers); Maire (delegates contracting authority, monitors, no a-priori opinion); STDE (technical support); AR/ERAR (technical support and supervision).

**Procurement thresholds:**
- **Services (intellectual):** Individual Consultant Selection / Qualification (CI/QC) by comparing 3 candidates — **whatever the amount**.
- **Works — Local Open Tender (AOL):** ≥ 20,000,000 FCFA · **Local Contractor Consultation (CEL):** 500,000 to < 20,000,000 · **Direct purchase:** 0 to < 5,000,000.
- **Supplies/equipment — AOL:** ≥ 20,000,000 · **Local Supplier Consultation (CFL):** 500,000 to < 20,000,000 · **Direct purchase:** 0 to < 5,000,000.

Process (Step 9, ~15 detailed sub-steps) runs from setting up CTMO/CEO and training, through launching consultation, receiving/opening/evaluating bids, award notification (with a complaint waiting period), contract signature, ERAR/AR approval and tax registration, to issuing the work order (OS) and site handover. **19 procurement forms (P1–P19).**

## Tâches du facilitateur (checklist)

- [ ] Aider à mettre en place le CTMO et la CEO ; former le B-CDV à la passation.
- [ ] (FT) Finaliser les aspects techniques du DAOL ; rester observateur à l'évaluation.
- [ ] Veiller au respect des seuils et de la transparence (public, plis fermés, PV signés).
- [ ] Suivre notification → contrat → approbation ERAR/AR → enregistrement fiscal → OS → remise de site.
- [ ] Rappeler que SC/FC n'interviennent pas en acteurs opérationnels.
